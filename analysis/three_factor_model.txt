Three-Factor Model: Quantitative Validation
===========================================

Model: missing_rate ~ β₀ + β₁·mod4 + β₂·is_prime + β₃·log(ring_pairs)

Data source: Full reconstructed counts from D4 analysis (n=7-22)
Method: Weighted least squares (weights = full_total / mean)

Results:
--------
  Intercept:  16.09  (baseline missing rate)
  mod4:        0.19  (very weak — mod4 effect is indirect, not direct)
  is_prime:    4.08  (prime n have ~4% higher missing-center rate)
  log(rp):    -2.29  (more constraints → lower rate)
  R-squared:  0.620  (62% of variance explained)

Interpretation:
  - The model captures a meaningful portion of the variation
  - mod4 has a surprisingly small direct effect — its influence is 
    mediated through ring_pairs (which grows with n)
  - Primes show higher missing rates than composites at similar n
  - The negative log(ring_pairs) coefficient shows that denser 
    constraint graphs suppress missing-center solutions
  - 38% unexplained variance suggests additional factors (e.g., 
    number of BB vs GG subgrid rings, 2-per-row constraint details)

For a more refined model, consider:
  1. Separate models for odd vs even n
  2. Interaction terms (mod4 × is_prime)
  3. Non-linear terms (n², n³)
